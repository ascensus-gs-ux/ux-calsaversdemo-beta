CalSavers for Employers

CalSavers is the retirement savings program sponsored by the State of California for private-sector workers who do not have access to a workplace retirement savings program. It was created to ensure all workers can save for retirement through automatic payroll contributions ran by their employer.

Registering your company

The State of California will contact you if your company needs to take action with CalSavers. The deadline to register or request an exemption is December 31 of the current year.

How to register

Follow these 3 simple steps to get started and manage your program.

  • Step 1:

    Register or request an exemption for your company

    You will just need your company's Federal Employer Identification Number or Tax Identification Number (EIN/TIN), your California Employer Payroll Tax Account Number (from EDD) and your CalSavers Employer Access Code to register or request an exemption. Request an Access Code if you do not have one.

  • Step 2:

    Add employee information

    After registering, you will have up to 30 days to complete your account setup by uploading your employee roster of eligible employees. CalSavers will then notify your employees about the program and their options as participants, initiating a 30-day decision window. After that 30-day period, you will begin submitting contributions for all participating employees.

    While you wait for employee enrollment, now is a good time to begin communicating with your employees. You can download a communication template to inform your employees that your company is joining CalSavers. You can also find additional information and resources to share in multiple languages here.

    Employee contributions

    After the 30-day decision window ends, log in to your employer portal and go to the Employee section to view which employees are participating and their contribution rates. You will then send the corresponding contribution amounts each pay period based on those elections. Contribution rates may change over time, and you will be notified of any updates based on your notification preferences.

  • Ongoing:

    Maintaining your payroll

    On an ongoing basis, you are required to keep your account up to date by adding new employees within 30 days of hire or eligibility, marking employees inactive when they leave or are terminated, and processing contributions for participating employees.

    If employees update their contribution rate or opt out, you'll receive a notification in the portal and by email prior to each pay period so you can adjust payroll deductions accordingly.

Designed with you in mind

Employers play an important role in helping workers save for retirement. While CalSavers was created to support employees, it is also designed to be as easy as possible for all employers.

Easy facilitation

Employers serve a limited role: run the program by adding and maintaining their employee roster and submitting contributions via simple payroll deduction.

No employer fees

There are no employer fees and employers do not make contributions to employee accounts.

Ongoing support

You have access to templates, resources, and one-on-one support to help you manage your role so that you can focus on running your business.
