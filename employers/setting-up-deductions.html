Setting up Deductions

After the 30-day opt-out period has ended, you will receive an email from CalSavers reminding you to submit employee contributions. But first you will need to set up your CalSavers deductions.

Looking for a more hands-off approach?

Payroll integration options can help automate the ongoing tasks of sending contributions and making updates when employees update their contribution rates.

Instructions to set up employee deductions

1

Create a payroll deduction

In your payroll system, or file, create a deduction labeled "Retirement Savings Deduction".

  • CalSavers is a Roth IRA program. If your payroll provider offers a Roth IRA deduction option, use that.
  • If a Roth IRA option isn't available, create an "Other" after-tax deduction option instead.

2

Review employee contribution rates

Log in to the Employer Portal and open the Contributions Dashboard to review employee contribution elections.

Log In

Important: Employees are automatically enrolled at a 5% contribution rate unless they:

  • Opt out during the decision window, or
  • Log in to their account and choose a different contribution rate.

3

Verify deduction settings

Because CalSavers contributions are made after tax:

  • Do not report contributions on the employee's W-2.
  • Confirm that the contribution percentage is calculated using the employee's gross income, not net pay.

Before processing payroll double-check that the deduction is set up as an after-tax deduction and is based on gross income.
